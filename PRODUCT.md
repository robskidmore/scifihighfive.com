# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Curious readers and listeners who follow sci-fi adjacent media (books, film, games, weird tech, speculative ideas) and want a home base for the show that feels made by people, not a CMS. They arrive from a podcast app, a newsletter link, a friend's text, or a search for a specific episode. They're literate, slightly skeptical of polish, and rewarded by craft and personality. They expect to feel the hosts' chemistry on the page the way they hear it in the audio.

## Product Purpose

scifihighfive.com is the home base for the podcast and newsletter. It exists to give the work a real address (not a Spotify page, not a Substack subdomain), to make episodes and posts findable years later, and to feel unmistakably like *this* show, not a publication template. Success is a stranger landing on a page, knowing within seconds what this is and who's behind it, and wanting to stay.

## Brand Personality

Warm, curious, hand-made. Sci-fi without solemnity. The "high five" is load-bearing: this is a show that finds wonder funny and shares it generously. There are multiple hosts, and the site should feel like a collaboration, not a single-voice byline. Voice is conversational, specific, slightly chaotic in a way that feels intentional. Plays with type and layout the way zine makers would, not the way a brand consultant would.

## Anti-references

- Default Substack / Medium / Ghost: centered serif column, top nav, indistinguishable from every other publication.
- Corporate / SaaS-cream marketing: soft pastels, gradient hero, identical card grids, founder energy.
- Y2K / vaporwave / neon-on-black: glitch effects, chrome type, synthwave palette. Sci-fi without the cliches.
- Slick podcast network sites: gradient hero with three featured shows in a card row.

## Design Principles

1. **Made by hands, not a template.** Asymmetry, idiosyncrasy, and small surprises are features. If a layout decision could appear identically on three other sites, redo it.
2. **The page IS the show.** Visual voice should match the audio voice. A listener should recognize the personality before reading a word.
3. **Specificity over polish.** A weird, specific choice beats a safe, generic one. Lean into the bit.
4. **Type as material.** Mix sizes, weights, and faces with intent. Treat headlines, pull quotes, and captions as different objects, not the same object scaled.
5. **Earn every element.** No filler hero, no card grid for its own sake, no decorative everything. If it doesn't serve the show, cut it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG 2.2 AA for contrast and interaction.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` strictly: zine energy can include motion, but it must opt-in via that media query.
- Episode pages need transcripts (separate task, but the design must accommodate them as first-class content, not a footer link).
- Type hierarchy and measure must hold up at 200% zoom.
